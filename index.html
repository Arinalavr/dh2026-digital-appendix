DH2026 Digital Appendix

Reimagining an Abandoned Coastal Social Space Through Community-Driven Speculative Design and Digital Small-Data Methods

A case study of a post-tourist hotel in Montenegro

This page is a stable digital appendix for the DH2026 poster. It will be updated with 3D/360 documentation, selected workflow materials, code, and extended findings.

Abstract

Across many post-tourist regions in Southern Europe, abandoned coastal buildings continue to occupy the landscape as striking visual markers, yet remain overlooked in conversations about urban futures. This project explores how such spaces might be reimagined not through top-down redevelopment or preservation frameworks, but through participatory, small-scale digital methodologies that foreground community voices, localized knowledge, and emerging cultural practices.

The research focuses on an unused seaside hotel in Montenegro: a structure positioned at the edge of a thriving summer beach where seasonal cafes, swimmers, sunbathers, and passersby generate a vibrant social ecosystem, while the building behind them remains largely inactive. Despite its disuse, the hotel has become an informal stage for creative interventions, particularly contemporary street art and temporary artistic practices that gradually reinterpret its identity.

Situated at the intersection of digital humanities, social sciences, and urban studies, the project combines qualitative interviewing, small-data digital analysis, and speculative 3D modeling to shape a community-rooted discussion about the site's possible futures. The poster presents the research framework, preliminary findings, and an evolving digital model, inviting discussion on how digital methods can support inclusive imagining of future use for spaces outside traditional heritage, tourism, or development narratives.
